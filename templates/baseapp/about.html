Welcome to 'SUNBIZ'

While you might only be interested in learning how to create nice maps and figures for presentations, reports and publications, it is worth remembering that many biological research projects inherently have a spatial component that is worth exploring. This can range from the distribution of sampling sites to survey tracks, movements of individual animals and information about the distribution of specific habitat types.

As a result, many biological research projects would benefit from the creation of a GIS to explore spatial relationships within and between the data. In particular, while some projects can be done without using a GIS, many will be greatly enhanced by using it. The very act of creating a GIS will make you think about the spatial relationships within your data, and will help you formulate hypotheses.
